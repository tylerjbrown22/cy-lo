Welcome to the club of strong women who have spent part of their pregnancy lying on a couch, bed or in a hospital bed helping their baby incubate. Although it contains the word rest, there is often nothing restful about bed rest. It is often an emotional, stressful time for moms and their support team.

Bed Rest Mom is about providing support to women going through this journey from women who have been in your place. You are not alone. There is wisdom from other women to help you through this stage of your pregnancy.

About Bed Rest Mom

The first time I heard the words bed rest I had no idea what it meant. An intensive search of Google only confused me more. As I tried to figure out what my three-months of impending bed rest meant, I also had to understand the realities of my high risk pregnancy, which could be life threatening.

I felt completely alone and confused. Fast forward to my second pregnancy bed rest. This time I was told I would be spending the remaining two months of my pregnancy in a hospital bed. So much for my feeling of been there, done that. Now I was facing a bed rest experience that was new to me, and in a city over an hour from my family. I also had two high-risk conditions to worry about. The isolation and stress had just doubled.

During my hospitalization I met some amazing women who were also spending their time incubating. In the 43 days I spent in antenatal followed by 14 days with my son in NICU, I learned the true value of having a female support group.

Whether or not you have the support of family and friends, your bed rest experience is unique to you. You are likely wrestling with fears and questions with no one to talk to.

I hope that Bed Rest Mom will help answer some of your questions and give you a glimpse of the journey ahead. If nothing else, know that you are not alone.
